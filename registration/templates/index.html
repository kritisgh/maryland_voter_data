More Marylanders Are Registering to Vote — What About Your County?

An analysis of Maryland Board of Elections data shows that registration increased in nearly every county between 2020 and 2024. Explore statewide voter registration trends or find your county using the dropdown menu.

Our site is dedicated to showing the number of Maryland voters who were registered to vote and showing how active voters are at certain times during the year.

This site in particular focuses on registration during the presidential election season. Maryland has historically been a blue state. The last time a Republican won this state in a presidential election was George H.W. Bush in 1988. Baltimore was the bluest county in Maryland, with Garret County being the most red.

In order to be eligible to vote in the state of Maryland, you must be a Maryland resident. At least 16 years old to register, and turn 18 by the election. You can not have been convicted of voting fraud, under guardianship for mental disability and ruled unable to vote, or be imprisoned for a felony.



The 2024 election hit Democrat registration harder than Republicans’

The number of registered Democrats dropped after the 2024 general election — much more than the drop for registered Republicans. There were 20,000 less registered Democrats in February 2025 than November of 2024, meaning about a 1% decrease. But for Republicans, that figure was roughly 0.1%.


The number of Maryland inactive voters — defined as a registered voter who has not voted in the last two election cycles — has trended upwards since 2006. While it has consistently followed the same pattern of rising leading up to the election and dropping off after November, the months following the 2024 general election marked a peak in the total count, which could be a sign Maryland voters are becoming more politically apathetic.
