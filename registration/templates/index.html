Maryland Voter Registration Dashboard

Our site is dedicated to showing the number of Maryland voters who were registered to vote and showing how active voters are at certain times during the year. This site in particular focuses on registration during the presidential election season. Maryland has historically been a blue state. The last time a Republican won this state in a presidential election was George H.W. Bush in 1988. Baltimore was the bluest county in Maryland, with Garret County being the most red. In order to be eligible to vote in the state of Maryland, you must be a Maryland resident. At least 16 years old to register, and turn 18 by the election. You can not have been convicted of voting fraud, under guardianship for mental disability and ruled unable to vote, or be imprisoned for a felony.
